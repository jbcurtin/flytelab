Not Recommended
87.4 hrs last two weeks / 87.4 hrs on record (51.7 hrs at review time)
Posted: Mar 2 @ 8:48am
Updated: Mar 3 @ 6:13am

This game is near perfect and I urge everyone play it. Never had a game in my life where I sat on the PC for 14 hours straight and not realize what time it is until the sun rise.

Why a thumbs down then? Because FromSoft still gives no f*cks about keyboard and mouse users. You can't use the map with the mouse, you can't control the camera while a dialouge is going on and so on. The game also does not launch on some PCs and even if it does, it suffers from specific FPS issues that consoles don't.

Edit: I finished DS 3 without getting hit with pyromancy only. Don't teach me how to play souls games nor its ''intended'' controller to play with. KBM is superior in most ways.

I voted ''no'' as recommendation because of the game's technical issues. It is not acceptable to publish a game that does not even launch for some people. It was always the destiny of KBM users to figure out how to get the most out they can; that part is only a rant, not why I did not recommend the game.

@Tequila Mockingbird "Why are idiots saying "this game wasn't meant for KB&M"?"

Because it wasn't, simple as. As much as driving sims or flight sims are, respectively, meant to be played with a wheel and a flight stick, Soulsborne are perfectly mapped to a controller layout, with the equipment cross replicating the shape of a d-pad or the left and right shoulder buttons controlling the functions of left and right arms, to name the most blatant examples.
I personally don't think expecting at least a workable KBM configuration to be a worthless demand, but expecting it to not be sub-par compared to a controller is preposterous. The fact that KBM can pretty much surrogate any kind of input device just due to its sheer amount of buttons doesn't make it any less of a specific tool for some specific tasks or games, therefore a sub-par choice for other kind of specific tasks or games. Unless people over here are also willing to argue that playing FPS with a controller is perfectly fine.
