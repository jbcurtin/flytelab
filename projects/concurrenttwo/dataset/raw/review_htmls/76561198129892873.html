Cyberpunk + Diablo. The game transports the Cyberpunk feeling excellently. It's dirty, it's flashy, with all the Neon lights and wow, this game looks absolutely gorgeous. Sadly it has performance issues with microstutters when using DX12.

Overall the game felt very good to play, I've binged it in 2 days straight, completed every mission in the game and was done after ~17 hours. The game is very short. During the midgame, the difficulty gets really tough. I played on normal and I am naturally gifted at games. Some boss-encounters though - especially the timed hold-outs around lvl 15-18 are reall difficult. Once I've unlocked the "Overload" augment though, it was a breeze. Duration as long as CD, so 100% uptime, increased regen on everything and most importantly: health regen after not taking damage for 1-2 seconds. Afterwards, the game was easy-mode.

That is one of the flaws with the game I feel. Almost all the time, the enemies you initially see on your radar is not the amount you're gonna fight. Enemies will jump from roofs or get dropped from flying cars. So when most of the offensive augments have cooldowns 30+ seconds and 1 use drains your whole energy, fights become deadly quick. Health management in the early half of the game is really tough at points. The game gives you hints, like duck behind cover and shoot over it. But the enemies simply bombard you with grenades, rockets, hacks or just teleport / jump right in your face from 2 screens away. Not sure how to beat this game without "Overload" - maybe I'm not as gifted as I initially thought?

Weapons are plentiful, there are a lot of guns to choose from - but: you will have to choose 2 or 3 weapons to upgrade fully: you will simply run out of components. The game has a basic upgrade system. You can upgrade your guns from MK 1 to MK 10 by spending components. MK 1 - 5 will use Basic Component, MK 6-8 will use Advanced Component and MK 9-10 will use Superior Component. At a certain point, once you've progressed far enough, you will find Basic Components rarely. And that is a problem. It costs a total of 11 basic components to upgrade your gun from MK 1-5. Some guns you will only unlock by completing higher tier missions or just by progressing the game. However, every gun you unlock is MK 1 at first. Which means at a certain point you will have your main gun and stick with it, because no MK 1 gun can compete with a MK 10 gun. This is a bit frustrating, because usually in games when you find a new gun, it usually means you've found an upgrade. Not in this game though. Bit weird for an ARPG to be balanced that way.

The game has a lot of bugs. I fell through the map twice, Skins for guns and shirts disappear after restarting the game, only the one applied will remain in your inventory. Some side-quests can't be completed because you need to progress the main story to a certain point - the game fails to mention that at all. And it's not intuitive either. Side-quest has a lvl requirement of 2, but to complete it you need to push the main story to around lvl 10, because only then, the new area you need to access gets unlocked???

You can equip 3 pieces of armor and thankfully you can hide the helmet. However there is no real variance. Found a piece that looks cool? Tough luck in 5 levels you will replace it or die all the time. Why there is no function to upgrade armor, I don't understand.

Money. Money is a joke in this game, because there is bloody nothing to do with it. I had over 800k at the end. Buying new guns? Pointless, see my point above. I had all the augments, all tactical gear, what is the point of money in this game? Why not give me the option to buy components to upgrade weapons for ludicrous prices like 50k for superior, 25k for advanced, 10k for basic component?

So much negative, why do I recommend this game? Because it is awesome to play. The gameplay is the big strength. All the time, something is blowing up, there is lights flashing, explosions, the sound is awesome, the music is awesome. The IMMERSION is awesome. If you specc certain skills the look of your character will change. You can turn from a normal human to a completely chromed, augmented super-human. You can completely change your appearance for free at a doctor at any time. Every major HUB has such a doctor. The game has a subway system, you can call a Taxi for quick transport. Everything you know from other ARPGs is in the game - without taking away or breaking immersion. Waypoints = Subwaystations, Taxi = Teleport to Town. This is so well done and amazing.

Also: there is no cops in the game. The game also explains very well why. This world you play in is owned by a mega corp. Without revealing too much of the very good story: the mega corp goes kaputt and the security ♥♥♥♥♥ off, because they don't give a crap anymore. They don't put their lives on the line if they don't get paid. So at points you will kill civilians, but it's no worry. Sometimes your superior will criticize you for your bloodthirsty behaviour, so the devs are well aware of what they did.

Overall the game is a 7/10 (8/10 if they fix the performance issues and bugs and rebalance some augments). Questionable design choices, bugs and simply too little content take away from an otherwise super amazing cyberpunk game. I hope the devs will get big money soon and we can expect "The Ascent 2" with quadruple the content and no bugs on release. Well done devs, this game is a gem.

So this is it, eh? I've had such a rollercoaster of emotions after playing this game. I was so hyped for the game. And I've played like 20 hours straight after release. I've chosen female V with female voice and Streetkid background.

There were some things that irked me in the beginning, like the 6 month montage of us doing stuff with jackie instead of experiencing it. But then the intro mission (getting that bot from the maelstrom) started and I was like: "♥♥♥♥ yeah this feels a lot like the demo. This is going to be so great." And it was for a long while. I was immersed by the incredible story telling. Showing the game in first person was the best decision ever. I've never experienced storytelling on a level quite like Cyberpunk. In that regard the game is a freakin masterpiece. You can read the emotions on the faces of the characters. The voice acting is top notch for every relevant character. In that regard, the game blew me away and exceeded my expectations. I won't go into too much detail as I don't want to spoil anything. But damn, that story had me close to tears at some points. Some from happiness, some from sadness. Really amazing storytelling!

But... and there it is. But: the game is no RPG. It's an Action Adventure. The choices I took, did not matter in the end. The only thing that defines the outcome of the game is how you decide in Act 3. Finding all cyberpsychos: meaningless. Finding all Taxis for Delamain: meaningless. Completing all contracts for the fixers: meaningless. Only side-content shown as markers on the map to make the city appear somewhat alive. I mean, I didn't expect a huge story twist. But it would have been nice, if finding all cars for delemain meant you could call him for a taxi service. Or if you've completed some minor fetch quests for your fixer, you unlock some kind of big heist as the finale to their "story".

Also at some point the game becomes too easy. I was level 50 and had a nice slashing build with Katana / Mantis Blades and some Cold Blood mixed in. I was one-shotting most enemies on Very Hard - not fun after a while. I must say: even though simplistic, the combat felt satisfying. Activating my Sandevistan and bouncing from enemy to enemy with my Mantis Blades felt incredibly satisfying.

Also I don't quite understand the open world aspect. I mean the world is incredibly detailed and beautiful. Definitely the most detailed open world to date. But it feels so shallow and empty. The NPCs walk around aimlessly, then poof or turn around and walk mindlessly in the other direction. It feels like the truman show. Everything is just a prop.

I don't understand the wanted system at all. I'm not someone to go ham on civilians so I didn't realize at first, but mistakes happen and sometimes you get a little to excited and hit some poor random guy in your mantis blade frenzy. I was surprised to see a Police NPC spawn right next to me. I decided to attack him, and then more spawned around me until I was dead. "Ok so no fighting them, just running" I thought to myself. And to my surprise that worked incredibly well. They didn't ever chase me. Well it's not really a bother to me, because as I wrote above: I generally don't go ham on civilians in those kind of games, but I can understand why somebody would be frustrated from this experience.

Next: the gangs are meaningless. They all stand around in groups of 3 or if you have an "Assault" Marker on the map there may be 4 - 5 guys. But it doesn't matter how many times you whack them. They simply ignore you until you show them your Mantis Blades in person. It would have been so amazing, to have them send death squads after you if you piss them off too much.

Ok, don't want this to get any longer. All in all, despite the flaws, despite the bugs. For me this game is a masterpiece and sets new bars for storytelling and character details / expressions / voice acting. It could have been so much more. But in the end I had a fun ride. If only there was any point in doing a second playthrough... Maybe in a year or too.

Until then, my only advise, if you want it: look past the flaws and play the game for the story alone. It's so worth it and you will miss out if you don't do it. Thank you if you read my thoughts. I just had to get them out there. This game has me emotionally riled up, partially from the story and partially from all the "could haves". Cyberpunk is a mixed bag for me. Some things exceeded my expectations and some things disappointed me. All in all for me the positives outweigh the negatives. If I had to rate this game right now, I would rate it 7/10.
