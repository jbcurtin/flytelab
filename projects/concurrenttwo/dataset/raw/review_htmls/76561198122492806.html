Alright, I loved this game when it first came out. I was very happy to pick it up, long with the other Star Wars space sims, in a bundle sell. I was going into this re-play 20 or so years after I initially played it.

I really enjoy flying around bigger craft, especially craft that shares a likeness to the Falcon or Dash Rendar's Outrider. It is a nice touch to mix between star fighter and big boy vessel. The UI in the launch bay is nice too, being able to switch craft and loadouts is nice and easy. Picking up, docking, and boarding are awesome additions to the space sim series. The story mission is pretty decent as it helps build the Star Wars universe a bit better with email messages and little touches along those lines. More importantly, it works pretty well in 2020. I'm very grateful that it still does. The game offers multiplayer, but I ain't touching that.

I have to say, it is frustrating that I have better controls and fleshed out graphics than Tie Fighter - but somehow worse experience than Tie Fighter. What I mean is that the voice acting is pretty harsh at times and minimally viable most of the time. The sound design isn't as tight as I'd like it to be. Missiles are absolutely frustrating to use as they never track unless you're very close and behind the target - which begs the question, why aren't you just using your cannons? Why do enemy AIs drive in a straight line towards you? Why does the campaign think I'd rather have fun taking out mines/mobile gun platforms than dog fights? Why is my targeting map jam packed with escape pods and ejected pilots - why can't I easily switch to the ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ dive bombing a retarded ally vessel? Oh, missiles do splash damage. Sorry Red 4. Lastly, in Tie Fighter they had a button to speed up time as you peacefully wait for slowbro to pick up something. These developers thought better and make you wait an agonizing amount of time to wait for more slowbros to touch their targets.

Even though there's frustrating aspects of the game, it works and it is fun. It somehow didn't age as well as Tie Fighter, but it still is an overall mediocre experience that will bring peaks of fun between the mediocre game design as you blast the ♥♥♥♥ out of tie fighters, gunboats, containers, mines and bases. There's mods and communities around still - so you can customize the experience more if you so choose to.
