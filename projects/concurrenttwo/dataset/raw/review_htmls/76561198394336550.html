Gears Tactics is a fun strategy game that is part Gears of War and part X-Com. That's been said by many and what I heard before the game released; but when you play the game it really is striking how the game still feels like a Gears of War title combined with the tactical combat from X-Com. Often brutal but always rewarding when you manage to take down hordes of Locusts on missions.

The lack of a strategy layer and randomized maps/missions might hurt long-term re-playability, but what's included with the game is very fun and the production values are very high for a strategy game. Hoping that Firaxis draws some inspiration from this title for X-Com 3 as I find it's combat specifically more rewarding to play.

Disco Elysium is one of the best RPG's released in years and has the best writing I've ever experienced in a video game. What's on offer is an intriguing Detective Adventure RPG with unmatched world design. The location and the characters feels very real and mirror the real world; and the setting allows you to sculpt your protagonist in ways not seen, or rarely seen before through unique dialogue choices and a combination of detective skills and skill checks.

I'd only be wary if you are not a fan of dialogue heavy games; but I'm not normally drawn into those games (I could never get into Pillar's of Eternity), this game had me itching to see every new line of dialogue or description of the world.

Please buy this game if you're on the fence! Any fans of old school style cRPG's, players looking for a provoking and deep story, or fans of mysteries or detective novels owe it to themselves!

Early Access Review
Noita Provides A Dangerous & Complex World

Noita is a super addicting roguelike platform-action game where you play a Wizard acquiring random spells, wands, and potions through exploration to battle your way through randomly generated dungeon blocks. The unique twist that every pixel is simulated physically; which is very similar to those web-browser falling sand games that you might have played with in the past. If you're less familiar, the simulation allows for very complex interaction of solid and liquid materials, so a stream of gunpowder that is ignited by an open flame could lead to a pool of oil exploding.

What Noita does well is actually building a game around those physics interactions with most of its mechanics based around exploiting and being wary of all the possibilities the game presents to you. The highs and lows of this game make it feel very rewarding while still retaining a lot of challenge. While delving down into the game world you might find yourself able to shoot a hole through a vat of acid, spraying it out onto an enemy or having your best run ended because a flaming piece of debris fell down on top of you from nowhere.

Fun game where boxes can fall and hurt people!
