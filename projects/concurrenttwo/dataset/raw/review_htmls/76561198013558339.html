26.5 hrs on record
I haven't finished this game, but after 26 hours, I feel like I've seen and played enough. I'll start by listing my major gripes.
1. The story for the past 26 hours has been mind numblingly boring, including the side quests.
2. The enemy variety is basically non existent.
3. The gunplay sucks. It's just boring. Weapons are surprisingly uninteresting given the potential weapons could of had in uniqueness given the theme, they don't feel fun to fire.
4. The melee combat sucks harder than a black hole powered vacuum cleaner. It's Skyrim/Fallout level melee combat.
5. The levelling and progression sucks. Pretty much all of your upgrades will consist of just increasing your damage without changing how you play much.
6. The bugs. I've seen ant farms with less bugs. Often, they happen during 'emotional' cutscenes, completely ruining the immersion and 'impact', which was already lacking to begin with.
7. The driving. I think if I dragged my nuts across a cheese grater for five hours, I'd of had more fun than if I drove in this game for one hour. It's genuinely impressive how ♥♥♥♥ the driving feels.
8. The AI. The AI in this game makes the AI in games from the 2000s look like they had achieved sapience in comparison.
9. The customisation. For how much they talked it up, is incredibly lacking. It's just a load of preset options to select from and there aren't even that many of them. This also applies to your V's personality. 'Tough, but soft merc' is basically your one and only choice after the initial life path that I've seen so far. I've heard that near the end of the game you get a few meaningful choices, but that's definitely not bloody enough.

And these are just the major gripes I can think of right now. Trust me, there are more. Over time, these major gripes mixed with the minor ones just ware you down over time making Cyberpunk 2077 nothing but a boring, frustrating slog of mediocrity where you look at what should be an interesting world and think of the potential that's been missed by a mile.

TL;DR: Even if they fixed the bugs, optimisation, etc - the problems with this game run down to it's core fundamental design. This game is the pinnacle of mediocrity. Would not recommend unless you've got nothing better to do and it's on a major sale.
