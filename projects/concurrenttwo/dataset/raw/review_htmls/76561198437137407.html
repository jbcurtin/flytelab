Don't let my hours fool you, I use the client outside of Steam.

Eve can be a lot of fun, but my god is the learning curve high. I mean really high, and once you really do understand how to play, you may not enjoy it. It's certainly not for everyone, and depending on what you really want to do, you might need to pay the subscription to do it. Honestly if you don't you're only going to get so far, as it is with most every free to play game out there, but it's very true here, as you're locked off from certain skills, ships, and equipment.

The game shines if you can join a Corporation and meet people. Good luck meeting the right people, as the people you meet is going to be a crap shoot. Tons and tons of corps out there try and recruit absolutely anyone they can, so it takes some time to find the right one that suits what you want to do; be it casual or serious, the corp exists.

It really is a massive game, and it's aged well enough; Eve stands as a testament to what other mmo games could accomplish if they have enough ambition. Eve is one of the few examples out there where the universe really IS shaped by the players in it, and unlike other mmos, it's all one server, which is a plus in my mind.

Eve is a grind like no other. Skills simply take time. You add them to a queue, and each one takes anywhere from a few minutes, to weeks, which really slows things down to a crawl if you think that it's just all about learning the curve and putting in the work. Its not. You go to the market, look for the skill, pray someone is selling it at the station you're currently at, and then you wait. Want to fly a specific ship? it's going to require knowing multiple different skills trained to various levels (1 to 5 if i remember correctly), and this is going to take you quite a long time to get together. Then afterwards you're going to need to outfit it with weapons and then outfit the weapons with ammo.

the point being, it's an incredibly complex game. If you like incredibly intricate games that require a lot of time and patience to get into, here it is. I recommend this game because I enjoy that sort of thing, but it's hard to suggest this game at the same time knowing the absolutely massive time commitment it is.

Best of luck

There are people who will complain about this game, but here's the deal:

Its free. As all free games, there is a pay to win element to the game, but everything is unlockable without it besides things that don't effect game play (skins, voice packs, etc).

it has some frame rate issues and buggy moments. the battles can be massive. MASSIVE. So if you aren't on a strong rig, yea, surprise surprise, you're going to run into some issues.

-It's a first person shooter with vehicles. It has something for everyone, and every role is very customizable. There are tanks, but your tank can be fit to take out infantry, vehicles, or air, and the same goes for every single role. Want to be a sniper? do it. Want to put on a cloaking device and get in close? you can. Want to command a platoon? it has that too. This is what the game does well. If you want to play by yourself, you can, but the game shines when you find a good group that has high cohesion.

It's grindy. Yea, its an MMOFPS, and like any MMO you're going to grind grind grind to get stronger... and where FPS MMO games differ from traditional MMO games... be ready to meet those level 70 vets on the other side of the battlefield. The game has no way of separating players into skill groups, so sometimes skirmishes can seem more like massacres.

All in all... this is what AAA developers should be working towards. Imagine if they made a star wars game with space battles built on this type of design. If your computer is decent enough, and you like FPS games but are tired to death of Call of Duty and Battlefield giving you the same experience for 60 dollars every few years, give this game a try.

It's a beautiful game that plays too much like an MMO and not enough like an elder scrolls game.

If you like grinding, and you like elder scrolls, by all means this one is probably right up your alley.

If you were looking for a game where you could have the playability of the elder scrolls games in a world where you could join your friends... this isn't really it.

Get ready to learn the best way of building your tree and which buttons to mash in which succession, assuring that in the end you're simply playing any other run of the mill MMORPG game.

As a person who logged countless hours into both oblivion and skyrim on console and PC, this game left me feeling uninspired after only about 30 hours of play. Where skyrim would have rewarded you for exploration, ESO only punishes you for finding yourself in an area you just weren't supposed to be in yet, which in my opinion flies in the face of the older games. Sure there were daedric quests and things with level caps, but I never felt like finding one of them was also putting you in an area where backtracking your steps and fleeing monsters far far out of your level was the only option for progression.

I'm sure its a fine MMO, it is not however a fine Elder Scrolls game. I certainly hope they don't stop making games like they did in the past to focus on this.

The glaring issues with this game are:

-Abusive admins who will roll servers back to save their base from being raided, or kick you off for any number of offenses based on their sensiblities (which may be childish and idiotic), and thats that, on to find a new place to play.

-Repetetiveness. The updates that Nelson (Dev) seems to want to focus on are small things and in no way generate new content. The code that the entire game is seated upon is obviously messed up, and it seems he's spent his time ever since trying to fix it. Every time he does change something, many servers go down until the admins involved make sure their plugins jive with the new changes. A lot of times you get weird bugs and issues, but hey, its a free game so you let that go.

-The real issue is finding your place. A lot of servers are pay to win, that's how they find the money to keep the servers up, which is fine enough, but when you roll into a new server to find 5 to 10 pay to win players running the server, your choices are to either find a way to befriend them, or get ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ destroyed over and over, and that is not fun I promise you. It's especially unfun when you realize those pay to win players have no need for your loot, it's just that there is literally nothing else to do for someone who can spawn in OP kits than to go around finding free to play people and destroying them and their base. This is a huge aspect of the game, do not underestimate it. You WILL log in, you WILL eventually decide to build a base, and that base WILL be destroyed faster than all Hell if you're on a P2W server.

So...

-Building is fun, if you can gather the right materials. The funnest part of this game comes from building a huge base and then fighting off a raid. The not so fun part comes from pay to win players who can spawn in lots of rocket launchers and tanks to destroy your base in 10 seconds when it took you 2 days to build. That... that can suck, but its a part of the game, which... again... is why i dont recommend it.

- So you find your place, a server you like, you even decide to support them for a few extra kits and maybe some swell coloring to your name in the chat. Then Nelson changes something, and the site owner throws his hands up and says "♥♥♥♥ it I'm done". I had a friend donate 50 dollars to a server that we both enjoyed quite much, for the owner to shut down the server the next day. He... didn't receive a refund, just a promise that the server would be back up eventually. It's been... 200 days as of this post. (update: the server never came back) The point here being: It's a game run by sometimes friendly, but otherwise unreliable people. I have no problem handing over 10 dollars in "donation" to a server to have some fun with friends, but I do become irritated when I do this and a few months later the owner walks away to leave the people who supported his server up until that point out to dry.

You can always take your business elsewhere... which is what I'm recommending. It's not a terrible game by any means, its... just not anywhere near what it could be in its current form, and that's too bad. Once the server I used to play on is back up, maybe i'll come back and see how the game is at that point in time... maybe Nelson will have released the next unturned game by then (which he is currently working on), or maybe he'll have fixed this mess somehow.
