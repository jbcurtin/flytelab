Early Access Review
This is a spectacular jumping off point for these devs. With great organic horror moments for spooks that feel earned, an appealing gameplay loop, and enough jankiness to be both fun and funny, it's an easy recommendation -- even in Early Access and at full price.

The one caveat: you need people to play with. The best that the game has to offer is found in multiplayer.

An obvious pressing concern is continuing support and the speed at which this game gets updated with content; the harder the spike in popularity, the more likely it is to lose momentum without updates to keep the community engaged. Furthermore, the "just one more" type of gameplay session that Phasmophobia fosters will likely contribute to how quickly players tire of the currently generated content.

Which is why the need for quick updates is so great, even if they're just ghost types and tools in the short term, with maps to follow in larger patches. There's so much potential generated content that just adding new variables -- if not environments -- will keep things fresh.
