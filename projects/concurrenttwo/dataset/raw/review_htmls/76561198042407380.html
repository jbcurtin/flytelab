Real-time tactical XCOM fighting Tyranids space zombies.

At it's core, it's a game about tactically surviving against hordes of enemies in a deserted Martian hellscape. Work towards saving the remains of humanity from the STROL menace and to attempt to reclaim the Earth after surviving Mars.

The campaign may give you the impression that the game is relatively easy and unenventful, but on higher Infestation levels, things get VERY intense the longer you have to stay in missions. Craft your Operator build with tons of different passive and active abilities, choose your weapons and prepare to run, loot, shoot mutants and run away screaming from the horde chasing you. Bring your shotguns, your flamethrowers, your plasma rifles. You'll need them. The campaign alternates between this tactical view with a VERY XCOM-like global strategy map that you have to manage the resources of to advance. You won't be able to save everyone, or do every mission, so make every thing that you do count.

On the multiplayer side, there's coop play of campaign missions that you can do with friends, or Skirmish single missions. That's what most people play, but I must mention there are very few people playing this at any given time, so filling out an 8-player lobby for a 20-minute Skirmish mission might be hard. I hear gaming sessions are organized in the game Discord server, so you can always try that.

All in all, it's a good game still in active development and support by the devs, with a fun multiplayer and a hardcore campaign.

Excellent racing sim with good competitive online structure and population. Easy to find friendly groups for league racing as well.

Very good physics, driving feels very good, if a bit easier. That also allows you to push your car to the limits even better, which is also a plus for me. Very good netcode, that deals with lag generally well, and does not turn simple car contacts into physics-and-race-destroying sends into the shadow realm. Hard racing is definitely possible.

Expansive breadth of content, constantly updated, and most importantly high quality and less expensive to obtain than it's main competitors, rFactor2 and iRacing. This, coupled with the initial free-to-play model, also makes this title more accessible to people who are dipping their toes into simracing.

Force feedback is very good and detailed, feels very *weighty* but still very informative. You can feel the car grip throughout corners and adjust accordingly. Of course, that means that yes, a sim racing wheel is required. Don't try to play online with a controller or keyboard.

And last but not least, some of the best car sounds in the market, every car sounds loud and magnificient, fills your ears with joy.

Early Access Review
If you are looking for a skateboard simulator, look no further. This game will provide you with that hard-earned satisfaction of landing the perfect trick to finish off the line you've been working on, and allow you to make a sick replay of it to share later. Twin-stick controls give you total control over your skater, even if it feels pretty awkward at first, the depth of things you can do is seemingly endless. If you can imagine a line, you can make it.

And it does all of this while still being in alpha, blowing the competition out of the water. Sure, it feels harder to get into, but the controls just make sense after you learn them. Many features that are only still planned or only available through mods on the *other game* are basic features well implemented here. Stats menu, time of day, soundtrack control, board wear, classic camera filters for replay editor... More tricks and possibilities. All things that are excellently implemented here. Darkslides, Primos, Late tricks. These, even in an experimental stage are already quite satisfactory to pull off.

The only thing I have to say is not very good in this game is skater and baord customization, as the variety of clothes and board graphics is a little bit lacking. But it already shows promise in possibilities, such as differently sized or shaped boards, wheels, deck rails. Another point that still needs work is the skater animations to make them feel a tad more natural and fluid, but honestly I trust these devs to keep pouring in love and work into this game.
