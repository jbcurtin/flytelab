Of course, this review is entirely biased because I've loved every second I spent playing the Dark Souls series.
It may or may not resonate with anyone reading it.

Personal Thoughts
It's a beautiful game, but I've had some minor graphical glitches, lag spikes, stuttering, and invisible enemies. Hopefully those will be ironed out in the coming months. Even still, I've been no-lifeing the ♥♥♥♥ out of this game--bugs and all. Exploring is rewarded with secrets, and full-clearing areas makes you strong.

Graphically, the game is Dark Souls 3. Storytelling-wise, the game is Dark Souls 1 if the story had 10x more budget. (Thank you, R.R.Martin.) Atmosphere-wise, it mostly resembles Dark Souls 3 with a smattering of Bloodborne with it's eldritch ♥♥♥♥♥♥♥ horrors... I'm 70 hours in and I still don't feel like I've 50%'d the game.
The world is expansive and filled with little details that almost everyone wouldn't notice, but I do; and I greatly appreciate the level of care that's gone into this game.

General Advice for Buyers
Yes, it's difficult, but it's NEVER unfair. If you feel like a particular situation is artificially difficult, you need to: (A) level up a bit (B) upgrade your chosen weapon[s] (C) adapt your playstyle to the challenge (D) consider whether you're even intended to fight a given mob at your level i.e use stealth and ignore it or (E) consider going to a different area--what you're doing may not be appropriate for your level/gear.

And believe me, some mobs just aren't meant to be fought. There are plenty of mobs that don't reward you commensurately for how difficult they are... Thus disincentivizing fighting them in the future.

Gripes and Complaints
Bugs encountered in order of frequency:
Stuttering, fps taking a nose dive, lag spikes, invisible enemies, lighting glitches, transparent textures turning black, and a strange window-rescaling issue that hasn't happened since I encountered it the first time. The majority of these issues are performance-based, and I believe it may be due to an issue in how the game is giving the GPU its instructions. Many people haven't seen most of these, so it's possible that AMD cards aren't affected by anything other than fps issues. Nvidia buyers beware.

General gameplay complaints:
Smithing stones aren't an entirely limited resource, as 1-4 can be purchased after killing a boss (1-2) in Liurnia and reaching a certain area (3-4) in Altus Plateau. In my opinion, as weapons' damage scales much higher with their upgrade level than actual levels, smithing stones are spread too thin... At least, due to the ability to purchase 1-4 being gated behind progression as far away from the beginning as it is. Even though I'm 100%'ing areas, I am only be able to upgrade 1 weapon to +14, and 2 weapons to +9, and a handful below that. For the area I'm in, anything under +12 is not appropriate damage-wise.
You're very much expected to experiment with weapons very early on and only upgrade a select few, but you're steadily getting new weapons throughout the game. You might find a weapon you like the moveset of, but are unable to use it due to low damage and not have any smithing stones to help it along. I had to help my friends through the boss for the 1-2 stone-purchase because their games were hamstrung by spreading upgrades across multiple weapons very early on. I was lucky I found a weapon I liked as soon as I did.

Final Thoughts
The majority of the negatives I've found in this game either a progression-blockade for NEW players only or performance-based. Even with that in mind, it's incredibly polished and very well made. Elden Ring is a love letter to the series, a culmination of the best features from each of their games, and includes a slew of new things that mesh well with the rest to tie it all off.

Behold, jumping!,
Praise the Elden Ring!
