WHAT IS IT?
It's a pure skill and reflex based game. There really isn't a thematic element to it, maybe a tiny bit of sci-fi.

PROS:
1. Very fluid gameplay.
2. Very addictive gameplay.
3. Leaderboards.
4. Simple to get into, difficult to master (especially when trying to beat the records).
5. Tons of levels to play through.

CONS:
1. Repetitive music

CONCLUSION:
There really isn't much to say about this game as its a very simple (yet innovative) first person platforming speed-runner. You have a huge number of levels with each one very unique. You also have many different paths to beat each level so its not linear at all. The graphics are "Tron-like" but fits the style of gameplay. Controls are very responsive and intuitive, you'll be able to get into the game immediately and it becomes very hard to leave it once you start. There is a sense of gratification when you beat each level and then replaying them again to get 3 stars. This game really hits the nail when it comes to addictive gameplay. Other than that, there isn't much else to say, no real story (there is "some" kind of story presented but its really nothing...) and the music is repetitive (only a single track). For a few bucks, this is totally worth it.

GRADE:
8/10

WHAT IS IT?

It's a casual point and click game. Thematic element is a satirized dystopia.

PROS:
1. Simple to learn, simple to play.
2. Relaxing, no stress.
3. Great time killer.

CONS:
1. Gets a bit boring as there is no climactic build up nor any engaging story plot.
2. No Steam Achievements.
3. Quite short at only a few hours.

CONCLUSION:
This is a quirky point and click indie game where you burn things, collect money, buy more things and burn them, rinse and repeat. There are tons of "combos" in which you need to burn certain things together and it kind of serves like a Achievement or Trophy of sorts (since the game doesn't actually have Achievements of Trophies built into it). There isn't really a story of substance but it is contained within the same "world" as the game "World of Goo". It's a decent time killing relaxer as you don't really have to think much but I wouldn't pay more than a few dollars for it as its quite short. Replayability is very limited as you probably don't want to go through the whole process again as there is nothing new each time you play. It's a great entry level game for kids though, no violence, somewhat cute, and easy to learn.

GRADE:
7/10

WHAT IS IT?
It's a 3rd person shooter with elements of RPG. Thematic style is science fiction.

PROS:
1. Beautiful graphics
2. Action packed and entertaining gameplay
3. A variety of puzzles that are each unique
4. Lots of lore and world-building, lots of lore items and background info items too
5. Great dialogue among NPCs
6. Elements of RPG mixed with 3rd person shooting
7. Lots of side missions

CONS:
1. Somewhat repetitive fighting
2. Most bosses aren't really unique, they are like regular enemies except more HP and damage
3. No multiplayer
4. A bit short (even with the side missions)
5. Some questionable spawning of enemies (and disappearances)

CONCLUSION:
This is one of the best 3rd person games I've played in the last few years. It's rare to play an action packed game such as this which includes TONS of world building lore. If you're a fan of being engaged into a games lore, this will accomplish it easily. It doesn't hand it all to you on a silver platter, instead, you venture out and pick up remnants and notes that will describe the world and the story to you. The level and environmental design also tell the story extremely well (many small details that you can pick up on if you look closely). The level architecture is reminiscent of brutalism in the 1950's and it plays off very well. The action is fantastic and extremely engaging. The AI of the enemy is well done as they take cover, chase you, flank you, and more. The RPG elements allow you to level up and research new combat abilities, equip weapon modifications, unlock new weapons, or even enhance your passive abilities. The graphics are also fantastic and they include a screenshot "photo" mode so that you can take advantage of scenes that make a good wallpaper. The gripes are very minor and nitpicky, and that is already me trying really hard to come up with some.

GRADE:
9.5/10

WHAT IS IT?
It is a bullet-hell rogue-like dungeon-crawler. Thematic element is sci-fi and fantasy (Lovecraftian horror).

PROS:
1. Lots of variety of weapons and characters to unlock and use.
2. Lots of endgame content that will add many hours to play.
3. Sound, environment, graphics, and atmosphere very suitable for this type of game and theme.

CONS:
1. There are rare bugs with some dungeons being generated in a way where it blocks you from progressing further (those elevators that spawn enemies).
2. Lack of documentation on item abilities.

CONCLUSION:
I'm usually not a fan of rogue-like dungeon crawlers as they become very grindy but this one is a rare exception. While not a Lovecraft fan myself, I did the games theme to be very engaging and eased me into it quite nicely. The dialogue and text were very suitable for the theme as well as the enemy sprites and environmental design. There is a lot of variety in mixing items on characters though by the time you reach the endgame, there really are only a small handful of items that are actually gonna be useful (super fast attack speed, super fast movement speed). The game does have a severe lack of details on item abilities (prefacing them with in-game text as "unknown") which I think would be helpful to certain folks that are going to optimise their build. There is also some minor bugs that affect gameplay such as dungeons being generated in ways that you can't reach the other side due to blocking. Most folks will have stopped playing after finishing the game with the first character (as can be seen from the Achievement statistics). But its actually the endgame content (after finishing with all 5 characters and taking on the elder gods as well as the secret level) where the game shines. You will get to make use of your most OP build but sadly most folks will never do this or even realize it. Look at the "hours played" by the reviewers here and you'll see for yourself.

GRADE:
8.5/10

WHAT IS IT?
It's a bullet hell game. Thematic element is fantasy and a bit of sci-fi.

PROS:
1. Fast paced action.
2. Good soundtrack.

CONS:
1. Terrible story/plot.
2. Frustrating levels (ie. not fun).
3. Controls don't feel fluid compared to using a mouse and keyboard.
4. Diverse item pool but largely useless as you'll be using just 3 or 4 of them most of the game out of the 30 or so.

CONCLUSION:
I'm usually not a fan of bullet hell games (ie. games that just shoot a bunch of stuff at you filling your screen with orbs/missiles/fireballs/etc.). I gave this one a bit of a try as it came with a Humble Bundle and it just made me hate the genre even more. Don't get me wrong, I grinded through the game and finished it on the hardest difficulty so I've experienced most of what this game has to offer. And it doesn't really offer much... a plot that doesn't pull you in, unnecessary item selection for the sake of having items, a large "variety" of enemies that mostly have the same strategies to defeat, and just some overall frustrating levels due to certain enemy types. The game does include an endgame mode which seems more fun than the story mode. The endgame mode throws you in a large arena where you'll fight waves upon waves of enemies while starting off with only your basic items. As you defeat more waves, you gain more money to purchase better items. This mode doesn't need to hide behind a weak plot in the main game, I would have liked it to be the main game itself and just scrap the story mode.

GRADE: 4/10

WHAT IS IT?
It's a first person shooter. Thematic element is apocalyptic future and horror.

PROS:
1. A very tense and suspenseful atmosphere.
2. Plotline is very engaging with lots of NPC dialogue and world-building conversations.
3. Action is realistic and intense.
4. Movie-like in-game cutscene choreography.

CONS:
1. In some parts, gameplay could feel a bit slow (due to the nature of the controls and movement).

CONCLUSION:
Yes, this game is quite old as this review is in 2021. But it still holds up amazingly well for an old game. If this game had come out instead of Half-Life, I'm sure THIS would have been THE franchise because it has all the right ingredients for a "Half-Life" game: horror, sci-fi, gameplay, level design, sound, dialogue, atmosphere, weapon variety. It even has some extras that would have made it better than Half-Life (if it was released at the same time) such as great cutscenes, and a more fleshed-out storyline. The atmosphere throughout the game gives off a feeling of dread and you'll be tensed up every time you turn a corner, or venture deeper into a dark tunnel abyss. It feels like Alien: Isolation, but with weapons so you at least have a (sometimes) false sense of security, which is what makes it even better because the enemies in here in most situations can insta-kill you (on the harder difficulties). Being based off a series of novels (and with a few games already, this being the first), there is much more world-building that can happen and I'm very interested in exploring the rest of the series after this one.

GRADE:
9/10

WHAT IS IT?
It's a classic style FPS (ie. Doom/Quake) with some RPG-ish elements. Thematic element is cyberpunk sci-fi.

PROS:
1. A classic shoot-em up that will pique any Doom or Quake fan.
2. Fast paced action keeps you on edge.

CONS:
1. Plot is not engaging and you will quickly forget about all the details of it.
2. Level design is terrible with lots of restrictions (ie. invisible walls).
3. No replay-ability.
4. Action is dull and repetitive.

CONCLUSION:
This game is a bit of a mixed-bag for me. I love fast paced shoot em ups like Doom and Quake, but this one seems to be lacking substance underneath it all (perhaps if it came out in 1997, it would have been amazing). It is definitely dated in terms of its underlying gameplay but some RPG-ish elements are added to it such as upgrading your weapons and searching/collecting a form of XP to get more upgrade points. The game is designed such that you pretty much need some of these upgrades to progress which takes away a bit of the RPG element. Enemies are a dime a dozen, all pretty much the same as in they just rush at you. You end up facing off against legions of enemies that are just trying to "zerg" you and you'll find that you are walking backwards and shooting alot. The action portion of the game basically boils down to a crowd control puzzle, trying to bunch enemies together and take alot of them out at once. The movement through levels is heavily restricted too, you'll see lots of areas that seemingly look accessible but really aren't due to "invisible" walls preventing you from going there which takes away from the freedom of an FPS. The plot is almost non-existent, described almost entirely during level loading. You basically forget the game had any story at all when playing as there really isn't anything to engage the plot during actual gameplay. All in all, this is a dull but boredom-killing shoot-em up which could probably get you through a weekend. Uninstall it once you're done, you'll never have to play it again.

GRADE:
4.5/10

WHAT IS IT?
It's a reflex skill-based game. Thematic element is political satire.

PROS:
1. Addictive gameplay.
2. Good soundtrack.
3. Interesting storyline.

CONS:
1. A lot of clicking.

CONCLUSION:
This is a pretty unique game and I can't think of any others that are like it. You play as a bouncer who has to check the IDs of all customers to make sure they don't violate any rules. These rules become more numerous and harder as the game progresses so you'll find yourself checking IDs with many rules against a clock (so you're also tested with how fast you do it too). This becomes an addictive element because you try to ace each level with minimal mistakes and the game makes it easy for you to go back to a previous save to do so. It's hard to describe the gameplay element without you actually trying it out, and the cost of the game is decent given the length of time and entertainment. The soundtrack is also really good with a variety of genres. The plot is a bit silly but there is a political element to it which satirizes the whole UK vs Euros thing (I live in Canada so I'm not as exposed to these issues, but I've heard of it). Thumbs up for a unique game!

GRADE:

WHAT IS IT?
A point and click adventure game. Thematic element is fantasy.

PROS:
1. Amusing dialogue.

CONS:
1. Terrible puzzles that have no logic behind them.
2. Story is a bore even for child standards.
3. Lots of back tracking means lots of dead wasted time.

CONCLUSION:
This is a standard point and click adventure game but the puzzles are definitely not standard. Normally I'd be OK with puzzles with solutions that rely on "outside-of-the-box" thinking... but this one is just soooo outside of the box that you might as well be an alien from another planet to understand the logic behind it. Even the character dialogue alludes to this by mentioning how certain things don't make any sense yet they just work. The background graphics are decent but the primary character graphics are terrible (for a 2009 game). Even older point and click games like Leisure Suit Larry, Monkey Island, etc are waaaaaaay better than this. The story itself is extremely boring and there is no sense of epicness even though it is supposed to be an epic fantasy. You spend most of the time in the game backtracking on puzzles as they seem to follow some weird order where you walk back and forth to retrieve items and it gets REALLY boring fast. There is almost nothing redeeming about this game and I'm usually fairly lenient too. Most of the reviewers on the front page here have not gone through the first hour or 2 of the game (you can check their profiles) so I would take their reviews with a grain of salt. I actually went through the game entirely so trust me when I say it, don't waste your time this... even if its free.
