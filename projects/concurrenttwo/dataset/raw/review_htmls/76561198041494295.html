Shards of Darkness is an improvement to the first game in all aspects and you can notice it very easily. The stealth mechanics are very similar to before but refined and the game plays better. The weak point of it is the story pacing and dialogue during cutscenes, it feels quite off.

I went through each mission twice and you can notice the terror that the Master difficulty brings, its truly an experience worth your time if you're a fan of true stealth.

Maize is a strange adventure game with simple puzzles and absolutely hilarious item descriptions along with great voice acting and visuals. It isn't a lenghty game and people might not like that it kinda holds your hand most of the time (hints are funny but maybe a bit too helpful) but it makes it all up with its story. The game doesn't take itself too seriously which is what goes really well with the events that happen in it.
I like it more than usual because the item descriptions and dialogue sounds like something I would write and do myself, the humor is exactly my type so this is actually my GOTY for 2016.

It might seem pricey considering it takes about 4 hours to finish but I can assure you those 4 hours are filled with humor, great voice acting and an ending that will be really difficult to forget. Definite recommendation!

What makes Aragami great is its ability to give you options despite looking very straightforward. As you get more powers, you get more curiosity regarding the levels you completed without those powers.

There are 2 basic ways of completing the game, as a shadow and as a cleaner. Trying to be a cleaner is difficult because killing and getting away with it is a bit more complex than just dashing through the level in the shadows.
A third way of completing it would be to never move. You give yourself a rule to never use the left analog stick and just shadow dash from start to finish. This makes the game incredibly difficult but my point is that it is possible to pass it like this.

It is easy if you want to just go from checkpoint to checkpoint but if you want to experiment your stealthy abilities and enhance your skill in the game, you will find a way to set rules and challenge yourself. As you get more powers, it gets easier but you can still set rules that will make it difficult and yet you will again be able to complete the missions.

The game gives stealth freedom that is easy, difficult, fun, frustrating, annoying and amazing. It is your choice how you approach it.
Easily my number 2 stealth game right after Dishonored.
