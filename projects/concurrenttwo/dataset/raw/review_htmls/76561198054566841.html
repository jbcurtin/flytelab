Had you asked me about this game a few months after release, or even just a few days ago, it would've been an absolute recommend.

Here's the story.

I beta tested this game and was instantly loving every aspect of it. Release came and went and I played my fair share. Taking screenshots of my stat screens when I did well, and just overall having a great time playing. I even bought it for my wife so we could play together. Had loads of fun.

Fast forward to 2019, 5/27/19 to be exact. I had a sudden itch to play. So i reinstall and load it up. All my character progress was gone. Everything had been reset. I figured something happened with the game requiring a wipe or something so I didn't think anything of it. Then my wife finally logs in to play and she still has all of her bloodpoints and items along with survivor levels and killer levels.

I immediately go to the forums thinking that maybe her account was bugged or something only to find out that my account was the one with problems. I shouldn't have lost my stuff. So I make a ticket as it says to do, not really worried because it asks for screens of your characters with points and levels showing, which I had, since I was taking screenshots every time I did well. The screenshots I supplied weren't "good enough" and I needed screenshots from my character screen, even though everything they require to be in the picture as in the picture. Yes because normal people who have no interest in streaming or making videos would have random screenshots showing their progress.

Quoted directly from the email

"Full-screen images of your Killer and Survivor characters with levels where all of the game's currency (iridescent shards and bloodpoints), your in-game name, and player rank are visible."

Everything they asked for was in the screenshots EXCEPT iridescent shards because there was no such thing in 2016.

All in all I lost 44-48 hours of progress. We put our trust in to these companies and this is how they treat us.

Now for the actual game. It can be a lot of fun. There aren't any dedicated servers, and the killer hosts the game. So if the host's connection isn't the best, then neither will be your experience.

Early Access Review
I really want to like this game, but right now I can't recommend it. The devs have fixed a lot of massive bugs in the recent days and even wiped a week ago. Gotta commend them for that. The effort is there, don't get me wrong, but even still after the wipe there's not much more than 8-10 hours of content and that's with extensive farming. Once you have a house and all the crafting stations, there's not much to do.

Would be really cool to see:
Randomly generated NPC towns that can be destroyed.
NPC Towns that need protection from local Outlaws
Named NPC bandits with Bounties on the bounty board.
NPC Trading Caravans you could rob and become and Outlaw.
Alternatively, NPC Trading Caravans you can be hired to protect.
Some sort of a land claim system.
A point to building towns.

Raiding is broken at the moment so PvP is pointless. Build a brick 1x1 with a safe and you're set. Which will literally take an hour an a half.

All in all, it's a decent few hours, but there's not enough here for a mainstay yet. Stay Tuned.

Early Access Review
Very cool looking game BUT READ THIS!! If you are in to survival game such as Rust or Ark or LiF or ANY \"REALEASED\" survival game, I strongly suggest you hold off unless you want to support the developers.

There\'s not a lot to it at this point(5/31/16), and it\'s very glitchy and buggy(It\'s in EA so obviously), so if you\'re looking to delve in to this sweet fantasy world, hit the pause button for a while.

Here comes the review of the game in it\'s current state.

Pros:
-Beautiful Landscapes
-Unique Twist on an Apocalypse Survival
-Little Robot Orb that Follows you around and carries your crap
-The building system is pretty intuitive and easy to use
-The survival gauges diminish at acceptable rates(Hunger, Thirst, etc)
-real time generation

The *In-between* (Kinda good but kinda ♥♥♥♥♥♥ as well...)
-Crafting... Literally everything. Rough example.
You want to craft an ax to chop some wood? Of course you do. To make that ax you need, a handle, a blade, a head, and some rope.(Don\'t quote me on the exacts here I\'m just making an example)
To make the handle, you need to make a shaft, and for the shaft you need a branch. Thank god you only need an ingot or some stone to make the blade, to make the head it was similar to the handle and rope was plant material to fiber to something else then finally to rope. Crafting was such a chore, but the fact that you could crafting things out of different materials to get different strength items was really immersive and cool.

-Anomolies - These supposed rare floating light shows with a box in the middle take you to another \"world\" briefly and you get to explore next to nothing. Granted they did mention they are quite useless at this point, but to be honest, the only thing I could see them being useful for would be to tell more of the story of what happened and such, but as of now they are just random places either a desert or a cave or something wherey ou run to the other side and get teleported back out. But the cave one you can at least get iron and they do have little crates \"from the creators\" here and there that have resources and such but nothing really note worth.

Cons... The bad... The crap that will be fixed first most likely, but still it sucks...
-Combat - even killing a little rabbit, albiet with a wodden sword, takes a long glitchy time.
-Movement - since everything is generated in real time it sometimes makes movement feel glitchy or laggy
Tie movement and combat together and it\'s pretty hard to play if you want to kill anything that\'s aggressive.
-Lack of Story
-Since survival is so easy there\'s no real driving force to the game.


TL;DR - Game has great potential. Buy It or bookmark it and come back in 6-8 months when they are closer to their goal as a MMO.

Early Access Review
Played it for free over the free weekend and had to have it after that...

PROS:
Beautiful graphics and detail
Custom load outs for guns
Decent amount of ships to unlock
Custom Skins
Space battles...
Strategy beats all
Micro Transactions aren't Pay To Win - Just skins and exp and credit boosters.

CONS:
Needs More Game modes
Could use more ships
Some ships don't have customizable guns
