Early Access Review
This game is in early access because it still needs some polish, but it's still very much worth playing. If you decide to buy this game, here are a few things that you need to know to save yourself some grief.

A: The AI has trouble against ranged attacks. Trying to make an enemy come to you can be hazardous if your strategy is to put your shields up front to block the arrows, as the infantry will frequently lower their shields for some reason. On the flipside, if you have a ranged heavy party, you can use this to your advantage.

B: When you attack a bandit camp, you won't be allowed to choose what allies you bring into combat. This can suck if you attack a bandit camp that has 30 bandits in it and the game decides that you chose to bring 9 recruits with you when you could have brought your veterans.

C: Press Left Alt to see important people and doors, this will save you from spending 30 minutes to an hour searching for an npc in the family feud quest.

D: Don't do the family feud quest. It terminates in a dice-roll that decides whether you are permitted to finish the quest or whether your time was completely wasted.

E: AIs, especially high level ones, sometimes seem to ignore basic rules of bannerlord's combat. Mainly, they skip certain animations in combat, especially the "bouncing back" animation that plays when an attack is blocked. Sometimes they also swing their sword straight through a shield, but that seems to be a glitch that sometimes happens in the player's favor too, as I've definitely landed some blows that looked like they were 100% blocked.

F: If you have a super awesome named horse that you got from an arena, and also have a crappy horse, sell the super awesome horse before you upgrade a unit that consumes a horse on upgrade, then buy the awesome horse back afterward. If you don't, the game will consume your good horse and there will be no way to get it back.
Posted March 30, 2020. Last edited April 3, 2020.
