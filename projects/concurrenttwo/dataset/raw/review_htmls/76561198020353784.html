I managed maybe 6 hours of playtime on the PC version of the game when it first came out and it really didn't grab me. I tend to be someone that's a bit burned out by the usual gaming experience, but the VR version of this game is a totally different beast. You feel like you're in the world of Tamriel and simple things like exploring, shopping, talking with NPCs, raising a wolf from the dead, etc all have a much deeper connection to me as the player. The game moves away from stats, finishing quest lines, and getting the best loot and more into just wandering, discovering, and seeking new experiences in the game world itself that you feel a part of. Hours can bleed by while you do simple things in the game via VR that you'd probably gloss through when playing on desktop because it doesn't engage you in the same way. If you want to step out of this world into a vibrant, living fantasy world then this game is your current best ticket.

On the down side, the VR controls are really rough. As in, they really didn't convert the desktop/console controls properly over to VR. This is a pretty big hit against the game and you'll see many complaints about this. You do get used to this though and it doesn't greatly pull you out of the experience. If I had one wish, it'd be that they'd patch in proper VR control schemes.

Another aspect is that you'll probably need to mod the game heavily to get it to where you want it. There are graphic mod updates that work well in VR, lighting mods that make the game properly dark, immersive citizen mods so NPCs act more naturally in the world, combat overhauls that help with NPC tactics, and so on that all help to greatly improve the VR experience. This is a bit of a pain to learn to do, but well worth it as it not only helps with the VR experience but will also allow you to better expand on the game as well for more content.

Performance-wise the game runs better than expected. I've been able to add in fairly good NPC body/face replacement mods along with world mesh improvement and re-texture mods without any noticeable hit in framerate. I've also seen very good VR compatibility with most of the special edition mods that are out there.

There's way too much inter-connectivity within the game for it to be enjoyable.

This is a pretty good VR shooter that gets a lot of things right and is rough around the edges on other features. You move slowly(though you can teleport instead if the motion makes you sick) through an indoor map and kill zombies and other undead as they come at you from the various hallways. That the enemies come at you in "lanes" with an audio noise before they start at you makes this game work very well. It makes it so you have to react on multiple angles of attack, but you don't feel like things are all over the place out of control.

The enemies are also fairly well varied with a decent selection of levels and difficultly. I think the game strikes a very good balance on enemy variety, difficultly, giving you "oh crap" moments without overwhelming you and letting you feel like a badass as you go all gun kata on zombies.

On the down side the UI needs work yet. Weapon selection is pretty bad to where I died more often to mis-selecting a grenade than I did to enemies. Also multiplayer looks to be pretty much a mess at the moment.

But flaws aside, the game feels like something you can come back to time and again for some casual gun play on the easier modes or an all out gore fest on the harder modes. It's a solid experience and well worth the price.

Early Access Review
This is a fantastic game which is basically Skyrim meets Minecraft. It has Minecraft's base building, crafting, exploration and structure building combined with some good PvE gameplay and the modability of Skyrim. You can build a house, tame a dinosaur and explore deep, dark caves in search of epic loot that'll make you more powerful.

The game has levels and a decent stat system with a lot of depth and complexity to the gameplay. Playing tends to focus on logistics as much as anything, to dive down into a cave you need to think about creating the weapons you'll need, the armor, enough ammo, maybe a dino or two as support, along with everything you'll need to haul all the loot back out.

If you enjoy thinking, planning, building and exploration/loot hunting this game will eat up all your time. If you're more into just pure adventure without wanting to deal with having to make the bullets for your gun(and the supply chain that gets you to that point), then this game might feel more like a grind.
