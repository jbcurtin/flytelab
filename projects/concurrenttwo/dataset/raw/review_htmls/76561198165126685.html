Early Access Review
I'm going to preface this by saying that I have experience in full contact swordfighting in a number of sports over about 12 years. I know how this stuff is supposed to feel, and this game doesn't really come very close.

My main problems with this game comes down to the AI. They are totally brainless. The most surefire way to win is by dual wielding and teasing them with one sword while you kill them with a thrust of the other. Even when you don't tease them, they will do ridiculous spins as if they really want nothing more than to be stabbed in the spine.

The weapons aren't great either. Even cuts with relatively low power to an exposed arm should make that arm practically useless or at very least hinder it greatly. Hitting someone on the top of the head when they only have a leather cap on should kill them or at least knock them for a loop. I know it's hard to convey weight in a VR game, but the two handed weapons are so absurdly sluggish that there's no point in using them.

It plays a lot like Xcom 2 with a touch of Breach and Clear or Doorkickers.

I'm playing on expert and that feels about the same as standard Xcom 2. It's smaller scale and the difficulty doesn't ramp up as quickly. If you are just getting into Xcom or want something a bit different, it's great!

The characters all feel well fleshed out and unique. Instead of having your standard classes, you will have individual characters who each have unique pros and cons.

As you play you will start to figure out some synergies between characters that work well. For example, Cherub and Zephyr work well together. Cherub can give Zephyr shields and she can rush in and smack some fools into the dirt without to much worry from enemy fire.

Ignore my short playtime as I didn't buy this game originally as I thought I could never get into it. I was proven wrong, so I bought it even though I don't intend to play through it again.

It's a little old and shows it's flaws in that it's combat is a bit clunky, it's graphics somewhat dated, and it's quest structure a bit too fetchy, but it's story really overshadows all of that.

I never really got into The Witcher as soon as I should have. It's story was on par with some of my favorite properties such as Baldur's Gate,

I had only one minor hiccup in my playtime and that was fixed by forcing Vsync on the game with the Nvidia Control Panel. Past that, the game worked wonderfully for a game of it's time. I had only 3 crashes, but my habit of saving often negated the issue.

If you can overlook the dated graphics and don't mind a few extra fetch quests, The Witcher will reward you with a engrossing and rewarding story that has some impact on the next game in the series.
