Early Access Review
Absolutely one of the most surprising releases of 2020 for me, found this game only a couple weeks after release.

Despite the arguably small amount of content available at that point, the core gameplay loop is incredibly fun, and the developers are frequently releasing updates, trying to keep on top of all the requests for more content, bug fixes and optimisations.

Gunfire Reborn is a rogue-like shooter, played from the First-Person perspective. Currently, there are 3 unique characters to play as, on 3 different difficulties. Personally, I find that all 3 characters are super fun to play as, all with unique abilities and upgrade paths, with each character allowing multiple playstyles to take advantage of their specialities.
I've heard people complaining about the viability of certain characters in the higher difficulties, namely Nightmare. However, I find that all 3 are powerful in their own areas in the game, so I think part of making it work, you have to learn what makes sense to do with the build you're going for.
Gunplay isn't really innovative, but it's very smooth and feels good during gameplay. The game contains 3 elements, the base of which is Fire, Lightning and Corrosion. Each element does bonus damage against a certain health type (eg. Lightning against Shield or the Blue healthbar) and has a chance to inflict a negative effect on hit. These effects (Burning, Shock and Decay) can mix with each other to form a secondary elemental effect, and they're all very useful so try it out!
To comment on the rng-aspect of the game, I rarely feel as if I've lost through chance , but instead as a failure to be good enough to succeed.

The music overall is quite good, nothing standout but I never feel like it's bad, it never really gets in the way of gameplay which is exactly what I like.
The visuals are wonderful, I love the art direction especially with the terracotta soldiers, the different dyed enemies all give that sense of a painted army, and their designs are just very impressive overall. In gameplay, cel-shading is used to outline weapons and enemies, but not the environment, which just helps a little bit at discerning between the background and mobs. It's subtle, but noticeable.
While models and textures aren't insanely detailed, I think the simplicity works in the favour of the game, allowing you to focus on the gameplay.

This is an extremely high quality game, worth a lot more than the asking price. Absolutely buy at the base price.

Warframe: A Great Grind


Quick Description
This game is my 2nd most played on steam (at time of review, I've played ~700 hours (steam says 832 as of current, ingame is different), the majority in the past year and a bit), and for good reason. The developers at DE struck a chord with their execution of the Loot'n'Shoot aspects, in nearly all aspects, with inventive weapons that are both powerful and visually impressive, with different weapons being built to harness certain mods more than others, but still allowing you freedom to create and modify your gear to your liking (within reason, of course (as long as you got enough mod space, you good)).

Community!
The community is quite honestly the best I've ever seen in any online game, sure it's not without it's fair share of people with bad attitudes, but the overwhelming majority are helpful and cordial. I've only seen a couple "toxic" players myself (albeit, I do mostly play solo or with friends only, so I tend to not see it unless I'm actively checking chat or doing a vault run).

Content, Pros and Cons
There's so much content in the game, which is a great thing at first glance, but does have a couple downsides that those inexperienced in how the game works, could possibly pose a "threat" if not shown how the system truly works.
For example, new players can often be overwhelmed by the amount of mods they have to choose from, and while it doesn't take long to figure out if you just sit down and put your mind to it, it can be quite off putting seeing so many little mods that seem to not do much at first.
There's also the market, and how not only is it one of the first segments installed, quickly showing new players that they can indeed buy gear and other items with real money, but that the front page is filled with a majority of platinum only items, giving the impression that the game is Pay 2 Win (which it very much isn't), as well as there being no tutorial about the markets main purpose, meant to sell blueprints for credits, and also show you potential items you might want to aim for, as well as places to find blueprints in case you can't buy them (Ivara for example, when you go "Blueprint", it'll tell you her blueprints drop from Spy missions), which makes it quite useful once you know what you're looking for.
There's also the login rewards you get once daily. Because they're randomized rather then tailored to your uses, a new player could get Tellurium for example and just claim it and thats it, have no idea what its used for, doesn't use it and probably can't, so it's a waste of a login. However, it could get worse, as potentially new players could get Platinum Coupons, which combined with the look of the market at first, could enhance the P2W vibes, possibly causing some to quit the game, which is a shame, as they possibly could've loved it afterwards.
However, moving on, the amount of content in the game is perfect for those of us who understand how the games basic systems and mechanics work, but haven't *done* enough or got enough in order to be considered a "veteran" player. There's nearly always a new warframe, weapon, companion, quest, event, you name it, for you to set your sights on, and make your goal. This is great, as it means there's always small, little goals for you to focus on that contribute to your main goal: Finishing the content currently present in the game. And here we come to our final "problem" with content. For veterans, while they may love the game, having finished everything currently leaves them no goal, no purpose to play the game except to have fun. And having fun can last a long time in a game like this, but there's only so much fun to be had until the "content drought" gets to you, and some sort of break or hiatus must be taken until more content is released. This isn't necessarily DE's fault, as it's very difficult to strike a near-perfect balance between content that's good and fun for all 3 player types, Newbies, Veterans and "Middle Men" (I had no other name) and satisfies them all.

Gameplay!
This is an mmorpg, yet it's also a thirdperson action shooter, with High-octane movement and ExPLOsIoNS!
The shooting feels very nice, and the placement of the character on screen feels like they're in a very good position, both in and out of aim. There's the obvious wall-peeking you can do to shoot around walls, which feels cheap and is always a downside for me in thirdperson shooters (when it's not "fixed"), but that's just my personal preference. Since this game is based around PvE, it's really not a "Dirty" tactic, I just don't think it's as fun personally.
The abilities of each and every warframe are what truly give you the edge over the enemies. Be it based around Offense, Defense, Support, Utility or various hybrids and combonations of these "Classes", A warframes abilities are what makes it stand out in various situations. There are also many creative (or miscellaneous, depending on how obvious you think it is to use an ability a certain way) ways of using abilities in order to do different tasks. One good one for anyone, especially starters, is using Slash Dash more for Mobility (It doesn't need to target an enemy to be used) than Offense, or using Loki's Switch Teleport on a downed teammate in order to make it easier for your teammates to get a revive (very niche, but it can be done). This, combined with the supporting fire from your guns and especially the damage from a well kitted-out melee weapon, makes you truly feel like an all-powerful space ninja.
And even if you don't mod out weapons to their most efficient possible use, you could do funny troll builds, because why not :) )

Levels
They're okay. Ever since the update to the movement system (Parkour 2.0), the levels have kind've been outdated. Especially the older ones, you can get around way, way too easily, and this puts a dampner on the uses of frames that used to be the OG frame for parkour (Lookin at you, Zephyr). It often feels quite odd, that a big, heavy frame like Rhino can just Bullet Jump and Aim Glide through the air, and at the same speed (or similar speed) to that of a very light frame, such as Nyx or Nova. But there are lots of puzzles and "traps" that, to me at least, seem to have been built for the old system, where they would've actually been a trouble and required us to actually make an effort to bypass them. This is especially true for a lot of the spy traps, however I think Uranus did quite a good job with it's spy puzzles, but it's lack of variation makes it easy to master, which is a down/upside depending on your perception.

Conclusion
This game is not without its flaws, but is any game? I think this is up there among the best, especially if DE can manage to enhance the new player experience and find a way to produce content that satisfies all, in order to keep players in, interested and having fun. This will be very difficult to pull off, but if they can, Warframe would easily become one of the greatest games of all time (If it already isn't).



TL;DR
If you gon play this game, either use wiki, watch some vids or have a friend who's played a decent amount help you on your journey. It'll significantly improve your experience for the first 20 or so hours.
