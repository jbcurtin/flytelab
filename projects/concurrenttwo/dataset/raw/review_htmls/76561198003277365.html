At first, I thought the game was a bit plodding and didn't like the feel of combat (which is the core of the whole game). After a couple of runs, I liked the combat more, but the game less.

The reason is basically just that the game is riddled with frustrating bugs.

Since the stages are all randomly generated, there are a lot of configurations where some caves are simply inaccessible -- for instance, you need to have the golf club to hit a ball into a hole which unlocks a door. Easy enough, right? But then there's a tree or a building or something that spawned right next to the hole and the ball refuses to go into the hole, instead simply hovering over the hole forever.

Another example: Some doors have two orbs you must hit to open the door. If you hit one orb to light it up, then enter a portal to another zone, then come back after clearing that zone, the first orb you hit is lit up, but lighting the second orb does not open the door.

Another example: If you use the tome of the dead on/near the same frame as you get hit, all sorts of dumb and bad things can happen where you get animation locked and can no longer move but are in the middle of iframes so cannot be hit either. You aren't soft locked because you can still access the menu and quit to main menu or (if you are on the overworld) kazoo out to a bus stop to get out of it, but it's very aggravating to have to just run through the whole trial again to get to the boss because the boss hit you in the middle of an animation and that caused the whole game to fail. This is stuff that should have been caught in QA testing.

I can't think of any other specific problems offhand, but these were the ones that annoyed me during 100%ing the game.

I did go ahead and 100% the game, so take from that what you will.

It's not a bad game, but it needs bugs fixed, and the game has been out for 3.5 years now so I don't think they're fixing them at this point.

I have the option of "recommend" or "don't recommend" and I would give it an "it's OK" if there was a choice for that. It's an OK game. Get it in a bundle or on sale maybe.
