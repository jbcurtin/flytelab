Dishonored 2 is more Dishonored and I think that is a good thing.
After the performance issues of the first week the game runs fine on my PC and I am enjoying it a lot.
There are two ways to play the game: As Corvo, the master assassin you took control of in the first game, or his daughter and now Empress of Dunwall Emily. During my first playthrough I am playing as Emily and have to say that the powers she has are a lot of fun. Shadow walk for example lets you crawl around a level more stealthily, and Domino lets you link two enemies in order to have their fatal fate shared. Of course the powers that were available in the first Dishonored do have a comeback here and there are all sorts of Bonecharms that give you extra perks, which can be very helpful to support your playstyle.
The other main character, like in the fist installment of this franchise, is the game world itself. The developers at Arcane Studios have put in a lot of effort to create a living and breathing world with interesting characters and locations that are filled with a ton of lovely details for you to find and fill your pockets with. On top of that the lore you find in books, letters and the environmental storytelling are once more fascinating to me, so I really enjoy to delve into the world of Dishonored 2 every time.
I recommend this game to anyone who liked the first Dishonored and those people who appreciate modern stealth games that draw an interesting world around its mechanics.
Posted November 25, 2016.

Human Revolution is able to stick to its origins, instead of taking the action-oriented route of the current generation. The developers at Ion Storm defined the gameplay of Deus Ex back in 2000, and Square Enix has managed to put the essential mechanic of the original into the new game: Play as you would like to play!
Human Revolution allows you to become an expert in the fields of your choice, be it hacking, stealth or simply your shooting skills. The city hubs occurring in the game allow you to take your own path through the questline, using the skills of your own chosing and will reward you with your own finale, depending on the decisions you made.
I think the game is able to unfold its maximal potential when being played in the stealthy way, but even if you like to take the action route, Deus Ex: Human Revolution is a great game that even has a great story, which touches on some interesting issues that are also relevant for our times. Thus, highly recommendable.

Rage is a good shooter. It has everything an oldschool gamer, like me, needs in order to get that certain kind of feeling while playing it. The guns feel great, the shotgun has the necessary kick it certainly needs and even the driving you can do out in the wasteland is fun. So, don't be mistaken, this is not the new holy grail for the shooter genre from id software. Nevertheless, it is great to see that those nerdy guys from Texas still have it in them to create an appealing first person shooter. In this case they were even able to add a quest system, crafting and some handy gadgets. Once you get the hang out of it you do not want to miss your new trusty companion - the wingsick!

This game is not for everyone. Brink is a competitive first-person multiplayer shooter. It is designed to be played online with your friends and not as a singleplayer game, so make no mistake, don't play with bots! However, if you are looking for a multiplayer shooter that does not focus on kills per death ratios or killstreaks, then this game might be for you. Gamers looking for something different and especially fans of the developer's previous game Enemy Territory: Quake Wars will find a game that wants you to play cooperatively with your team in order to defeat the other one. Brink focuses on map-specific objectives that need to be fulfilled to win the map. So if you're looking for a shooter that pushes you so hard to play together, that you will sometimes curse your teammates for not helping you, this is your game.
The most issues the game had at its release were fixed up until now, which makes the game highly recommendable to me.
Posted July 4, 2011.
