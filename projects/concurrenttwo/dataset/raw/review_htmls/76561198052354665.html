At the time of writing this, I have played for about 17 hours and completed the main story on Lunatic mode with 3 stars without the help of DLC. I have not done the EX stages, hero mode, or challenge mode; but I have played a bit of the new roguelike mode and used the DLC characters/towers in it.

I am absolutely loving this game. I love the touhou universe, and the ability to control a player character to attack enemies and pick up/throw towers makes this game really stand out. Lunatic mode is challenging but doable as long as you use both your towers and your character well. It's pretty well balanced, though stages with a major boss ramp up the difficulty (and also the Sanzu river stage). The DLC characters and towers don't seem to be better than the base game characters/towers, they're just there to give you more options and support the devs. For a $20 game ($30 with the current DLCs) I'd say this is well worth it for any Touhou and Tower Defense fan.

P.S. Hata no Kokoro character or tower when? I'd buy that DLC too.

Early Access Review
I'll start by saying I WANT to like this game. The controls are fairly smooth, and everything looks pretty awesome. The character creator is great. It's fun for a few hours as you learn how to play and how things work, but once you get used to things and are left with only grind, it starts to get boring fast. The waiting due to queue times - because of the low player base - doesn't help. A fair amount of my logged time is due to waiting on queues (also a few hours are from me being afk). The game also has a few bugs, but for the most part you can ignore them. EDIT: After hanging around the subreddit and discord channel, I've heard there are more bugs than the minor ones I've run into, and some can be quite problematic. Bugs might be a bigger problem than I've realized.

Anyway, here are the reasons I don't recommend this game in its current state:

-CRAFTING SYSTEM: It's *mostly* cosmetic only, but it straight up sucks, and could be the worst I've ever come across. Let me explain. To make a SINGLE item of your choice (weapon skin, piece of clothes, accessory) you need to break down 16 other items of that type (as of right now it takes 32 "essences" - 64 for some WTF- of a cosmetic type to make your chosen item, and a reddit post I read said that each dismantled item gives 2 essences). Now you might ask why I mentioned a reddit post when I have over 10 hours in the game and could find out for myself, but guess what - so far I haven't even gotten a single item that I can dismantle for essences. Oh, and I hear dismantling actually has a chance to FAIL. ??????????????

Now brace yourself, because it gets worse!

-LOOT BOXES: Right now there are two kinds - basic and advanced. They come in 7 types: Costume, and 1 each of the 6 different weapon archetypes. You have to buy loot boxes with in-game currency (which takes multiple hours just to grind for a single basic one or literally 3x as long for an advanced one) OR buy them with real money (oh look, what a surprise!) in order to get these cosmetic pieces to dismantle for essences (or if you're amazingly lucky you may get what you want, I guess *kekw*). AND IF THAT WASN'T BAD ENOUGH, These "specialized" loot boxes only have a small chance of giving you the type of thing you bought them for. The chances are listed below (note that there are 6 types of weapon boxes - one for each archetype, all with the same %chances):

Advanced Supply Box (Costume): 34% Costumes(top/bottom), 31% Accessories(Gloves/Shoes), Dyes 20%, Emotes 15%

Advanced Supply Box(Weapon): 17% Weapon(Equipment), 47% Accessories(Excludes Gloves/Shoes), Dyes 20%, Emotes 16%

Basic Supply Box (Costume): 11% Costumes(top/bottom), 20.5% Accessories(Gloves/Shoes), Dyes 66.4%, Emotes 2.1%

Basic Supply Box(Weapon): 2.7% Weapon(Equipment), 28.6% Accessories(Excludes Gloves/Shoes), Dyes 66.4%, Emotes 2.3%

I'm actually outraged at these chances; I'm also pretty upset that you can get things from a weapon or costume box that isn't actually a weapon or outfit. This is insanely greedy, and I won't be giving this game a cent unless they massively rework crafting and/or loot boxes. Oh, and one last complaint - there is at least one PvE mission locked behind PvP rank. I hate PvP in this type of game, and I'm not that good at it either. Who the hell thought locking a PvE mission behind PvP rank was a good idea?

Anyway, to summarize: KurtzPel is a decently fun game, at least for a few hours. Would help pad out the play time if grinding was actually rewarding, but it isn't. I hope they dial down the greed a few orders of magnitude, because I want a reason to keep playing this game in the long run. Running the same missions with the same basic gear over and over and having almost no hope of getting the cosmetic items I actually want really turns me off. Grind is good in moderation, but this is insane.

Dungreed is a fun roguelike with smooth controls and very good pixel graphics, which is important in a game that relies on replayability (you wouldn't want to replay a game with clunky controls or bad graphics again and again now, would you?). It has a good balance of difficulty and RNG, meaning that you can get far by skill even if your weapons/items aren't the best, even with good gear you have to be careful to survive until the end, and rarely you can let out your frustration by getting a completely OP build and annihilating anything in your way.

The upgrade system is a good balance between permanent and temporary progression, with the out-of-dungeon permanent upgrades (permanent unless you activate something that can be likened to prestige ranks in other games) giving you moderate abilities and stats, and in-dungeon upgrades giving you more powerful temporary (they last until you win or lose your current run) abilities and stats. While the out-of-dungeon upgrades are important, I believe it's choosing the correct in-dungeon upgrades that will usually make or break your run. Having RNG gift your the right gear is great, but picking the right temporary upgrades is what makes your equipment truly shine.

It's not the heaviest on content, but I think that the content it does have is done very well. As of writing this I have 49 hours on record, and I doubt I'll play it again for a while, but I believe that those 49 hours playing a well crafted and satisfying game are well worth the $10 this game cost me.

Here's how it goes: I was in a single game for 30-40min(I think I jumped in quite a while after it started), we won, and at the end I got about 3000 in-game currency. The next rifle (stock edition, no changes) costs about 63000 (or a certain amount of cash bought in-game currency). That's no exaggeration. Great game, way too pay-to-win though.
