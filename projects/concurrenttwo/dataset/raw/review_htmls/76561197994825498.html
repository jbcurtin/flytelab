A very well done remaster of the original first Sam& Max season by Telltale. I'm honest: Back then when I played the original I was rather disappointed by it. The graphics looked dated already, the audio was heavily compressed (in order to reduce the download size) and the first 2 episodes were quite boring.
While the story did not change, Skunkape certainly improved the audiovisual part of the game and it looks and sounds just great. This is a huge improvement compared to the original (which is by the way hardly playable on modern machines). Yeah, the first two episodes are still not the best, but at least you can play them in glory now ;-) I really enjoyed the whole game a lot more this time and I'm looking forward to a remastered season 2 (which is the best of the Telltale trilogy).

Overall a very decent remake of a classic PC game. In some ways it even surpasses the original when it comes to story-telling. There is no need to compare the graphics, as the remake is simply from a different era.
Yes, there are changes (art style, minor mission changes, etc). Yes, the music is different, and yes some of the characters look different. But in the end this is a new interpretation of the original Mafia story and I think it holds up pretty well. The graphics are amazing and the game is still quite hard (when played on Classical).
Minor things I did not like were some casual changes to the first mission as well as you basically can't play stealth anymore (at least one mission could be solved without killing anyone in the original ).

TL;DR: My personal game of the year 2017!

Ron Gilbert and his team managed to create an adventure game that is both true to the classics like Maniac Mansion and Monkey Island and yet so fresh and inspiring as you'd expect it from a game released in 2017. While the art style may look dated at first, it is incredible well done and very detailed (and finally we have ladder-climbing animations!). The story itself is hilarious and includes lots of references and homages to older LucasArts classics while the pizzles are nicely woven into the story.
Some people complained about the voice acting, however it was just like I expected it to be. The LucasArts classics which had voice acting always featured some hilarious (not in a bad way though) voices which added a lot to the game - just like in ThimbleWeed Park.
I've just finished the game and will definitely play it again (this time in casual mode, just to see the differences).

I backed this title when the campaign was live at Kickstarter with $30 and it is worth every penny!
