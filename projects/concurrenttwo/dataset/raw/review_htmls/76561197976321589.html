Recommended
56.7 hrs on record (56.2 hrs at review time)
Having given all or at least most of the golf games currently available on steam a fair shake, this one is my favorite. I'll try to explain why concisely;

- Simple, functional, easy to use multiplayer lobby system.
- Tons of downloadable courses that are as good or better than the ones that come with the game's original install. 98% are free! Just takes a bit of time and work to download them.
- Different control schemes available, as well as different difficulty settings for them -- this critical feature absent in some other big golf games allows you to learn and enjoy a course, the physics of the game, and playing with friends independently of your progress at mastering a particular control scheme or mechanic. This makes the game accessible, allowing you to up the difficulty yourself as you improve and learn the game -- players just need to take advantage instead of playing at too hard a difficulty level and then blaming "bad controls" which I see a *lot* of in other reviews below. Some seem unable to stomache that "Amateur" is actually a hard difficulty level just because of the word.
- Great overall gameplay & physics, good enough graphics [gameplay is just far more important].

Where it has room for improvement:

- Stability; game has crashed a few times at the end of a multiplayer round, and less frequently but a few times have run into freezing/can't-continue type issues in the middle of a multiplayer game.
- Speed/smoothness of online gameplay; there are endless "click to continue" type popups after every shot, every 9 holes and etc. This mechanic just slows down the speed of online gameplay and becomes more and more of an irritation over time.
- Speed/smoothness of online gameplay; there are just numerous delays between everything in this game and no attention at all in general paid to making the game run smoother, faster, and have any respect for people's time during multiplayer play. You should be able to at least enable an option to skip or speed up the viewing your own shots, and the time inbetween shots is just gratuitous sometimes. Even 18 holes with a couple friends can often be a 2+ hour affair.
- Progression? Stats? Career? Online ladder? Ranking system of some kind? Unlocks based on achievements and ranks?
- Why not incentivize using more difficult control settings via the non-existent but hope-for-in-the-future systems of progression, achievements, rankings, and unlocks?
-Looking through the negative reviews a majority of them are frustrated because they simply cannot figure out how to set the difficulty of the swing to a good balance that challenges them a bit without being overly frustrating, and can't figure out/can't-be-bothered-figuring-out how to download and install the many popular 3rd party courses, the vast majority of which are free. Why not integrate a course-browsing/rating/viewing/downloading system into the game? Why not integrate a feature that adapts your difficulty settings for swings to a point where you can semi-consistently hit fairways but it still leaves some substantial difficulty there so you can improve?

I'm really looking forward to the sequel to this game. Hopefully it can make the gameplay smoother, faster, more stable -- and even add some elements of progression. I think that would be a huge success and very popular. Until then this is still the best option for a relaxing game with a friend -- but have your coffee or snacks ready it does go a bit slowly.

Not Recommended
1.6 hrs on record
Game mechanics don't give a feel of consistency that allows you to learn the game and improve the way you would expect with Golf game -- too much RNG I suspect.

Not a fan of the controls with mouse & keyboard, I understand the need to have some kind of "skill involved", but other golf games have better ideas & implementation here IMHO. Would be fine with it if there was another option and/or some way of making the tempo of your swing not the primary difficulty & core mechanic of having a good shot.

Worst of all, there doesn't seem to be any obvious & straightforward way to simply start a multiplayer "lobby" and play with a friend; I'm sure there is a way around this but it seems convoluted and silly for what is to most people the primary way they are going to want to enjoy a Golf game on steam.

Edit: I'd like to add that the first impressions of the game were great, the graphics incredible, and most PC Golf type players that don't mind more of an investment in learning the game may find it to be the best out there at the moment. [But having the game developer come in and rate my review unhelpful for pointing out issues that many other people also have in their reviews is also not a good sign.]
