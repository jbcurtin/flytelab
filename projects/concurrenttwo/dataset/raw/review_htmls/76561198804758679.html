Early Access Review
30 hours in, I'm still surprised at the character depth, interaction, and gameplay loops this rogue-like has to offer. Supergiant really hits it out of the part this time with the music, character design, and snappiness of gameplay.

I've had more fun with this $20 purchase than some of the AAA titles that boast much more in terms of game-length.

If you're a fan of Greek lore, this game is a huge treat for the world alone, and the game does a great job at wanting you to keep playing more.

Some criticisms as of May 18th 2020:
- Some weapons are much slower than others.
- Weapon balance could use a little work, some weapon aspects are a little under-tuned.
- Boss encounters end up getting a little face-roll-y once you get comfortable with the game.
- Some performance issues with 'anti-save-scum' save system.

Not Recommended
4.8 hrs on record
Simply put, this game has terrible netcode and even worse balancing.

As someone who has played the first GunZ more than any game in this generation, I was initially sad to see K-styling go, but I knew it was necessary to appeal to a new audience. I had hoped GunZ 2 had the same action packed combat even without the fancy moves of a kstyler.

It turns out I was wrong.

Not only does this game lock you into weapons depending on the character you use, some characters are so powerful you might as well not even use anyone else. Take into account the powerhouse of the game, Elena the Gunslinger. She has an assault rifle as well as a grenade launcher. The grenade launcher has a mechanic if you hit one, your target is stunned for half a second. In that time, if you shot another, it would explode right next to them - essentially being able to lock you until death. Not only that, the assault rifle has a very small cone of fire, such that you are able to shoot and dash at the same time without much repercussions. However, I had the unfortunate luck of choosing Ivan (I like scarves), and to my disdain, he uses a sniper rifle.

A sniper rifle. In a game where you're always dashing. What's worse is that his SMG is completely horrible at anything longer than twenty feet due to its ridiculous cone of fire. It requires Ivan to move in close, and even then the dashing makes it quite difficult to land consistent shots. Why would I choose Ivan's SMG when I would rather use Elena's rifle? I would love to switch it out but I can't because weapons are locked to characters. This is a shooter, not an RPG game, Maiet has done a very poor job in allowing people to play a shooter as it's fundemental core. Locking guns to a character not only creates a disparity between them, it also forces people to adopt a playstyle they may not like and have to endure insufferable games in order to afford a new one.

If that doesn't convince you not to play, then the netcode will. The most pathetic thing is that the first GunZ had a BETTER netcode than this one. Sure, you had to lead your shots - but the character models were smooth and did not teleport. In this game, it assigns a host that people connect to. If any game does this as a shooter, they are bound to fail. This is not 2007, the only viable way to design shooters is having all players use CLIENT-SIDE netcode. If the host is bad, everyone suffers EXCEPT THE HOST. This game doesn't even have dedicated servers. Players rubber band, I end up hitting ghosted targets and the host gets away with having fifteen kills while I can barely hit anything with the latency and my cruddy SMG.

For those who would argue this is an Early Access... the game has been out in Korea and EU for nearly a year. Even if they fix the netcode, the balance will be still absolutely horrible. I search up videos of GunZ 2 in korea and I barely see any Ivans. If its taken them this long and nothing has happened to the game, you can expect it to happen again here on Steam. Any balance done to the game has to go through Korea first, and from my experience with past games they have a very poor track record in doing balance patches.

I am really disappointed in Maiet, and I hope this gets taken off of Steam. I rarely get frustrated to the point I uninstall a video game, and its incredibly saddening I have to do so with my childhood game. Pathetic. Just absolutely pathetic. Do yourself a favour and do not play this game. It's charm of running on walls and chaining sword and gunstrikes loses it's charm very quickly... unless you play Elena.
