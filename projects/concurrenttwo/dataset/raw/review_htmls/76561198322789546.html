Just beat the game with all the towers and outposts complete. It took a good chunk of time to do so, around 34 hours, so I'm happy that it's as long as it is if you do the side stuff. Because of a few certain weapons, the game can tend to be a bit easy, especially once you get around to getting the small explosives launcher, and a silenced sniper rifle, but that's how Far Cry 3 was too. Outposts are fun to take out, Radio towers are fun to climb, the Shangri-Lah stuff is a good change of pace and visually very pretty.

The buzzer though, the one-man copter, kinda makes the Radio Towers too easy seeing as how you can just land the damn thing on the top floor of most of them and complete them that way. Games fun. I really enjoyed Far Cry 3, and this is just more of that and since it's been long enough since I personally played Far Cry 3, I'm all for it. If Far Cry 5 is just this again though... eh I'll probably still enjoy it. Wonder how many iterations it'll take before it gets old though.

(TL:DR)-Game crashes alot. Sometimes at the worst times, setting you back very far (worst was an hour for me). If that weren't an issue, this game would be great. Buy it but get ready for frustration.

Before I fought the second to last boss, I had to wait and hear some dialogue while my previous boss kills were shown to me , alongside with the weapon I used to kill them, how long it took, and how many attempts. This whole thing took about 10 minutes to 15 minutes.

Halfway through that next boss fight, one of the bosses hit me with a flame attack while I was blocking with the gauntlet and my game crashed.

Since the game only saves the state whenever you hit a checkpoint or go through to a major zone, I got reset to before the 15 minute dialogue.

The game, in the state that it is in on 11/2/2014, is broken just enough where, when it does crash, it is extremely infuriating.

One of the mechanics in the game is, the longer you go without hitting a checkpoint, the better drop rate and experience you'll gain. This would work FANTASTICALLY if the game never had any real threat of crashing. But since it does crash frequently, and the game only saves on checkpoints or on entering a major story zone, you can very easily loose an hour of progress just because you were trying to raise your experience/item multiplier by not hitting every checkpoint.

This happened to me multiple times and the most infuriating is when it happens after a boss fight or after sitting through a bunch of dialogue or finding a key item. It's just ♥♥♥♥♥♥, real ♥♥♥♥♥♥.

The thing is though, I can't say how I'd review the game if this never occured because I'm just a bit salty over the whole thing. The game itself is solid, it's souls-like, it's generally easier than dark souls or demon souls, the design is much more fantasy and bright and none of that is bad. The combat has some nice mechanics (with the timing aspect and whatnot) but the bosses are a little simple.

You can make the game quite easy for yourself by making sure to level up your health and then stick to one main damage dealing stat (maybe a little faith for your gauntlet and spells, atleast to the point where you can use all your class spells) and you'll likely never have a problem. Pay attention to any buff icons that might appear on your character, because some class spells don't mention specific buffs (for example in the blue spells, the 'shelter' spell will actually heal you although that is not mentioned in the description).

I don't know, the game is fun, it looks good, it plays well, it's fairly short (not counting crashes, someone coming from a dark souls or demon souls will easily beat the game in under 10 hours) and there's some replayability in trying out different classes. Though I hear new game + is lackluster.

I haven't beat the game because having just crashed at the 2nd to last boss I'm just frustrated. I would have no second though recommending this game IF IT WASN'T SO BUGGY! I'll still say 'yes', in the hopes that at some point the issues are fixed but just be warned, this game is buggy and it can lead to some real bad frustration.

At about 22 hours I've collected all the collectibles, gotten all weapon upgrades, and nearly every skill. The main story is fairly short if and so you have to end up enjoying the combat and side stuff in the game in order to really enjoy the game itself. The side stuff is hella fun. Having specific goals and challenges (plus the tiny optional bonus goal) for the side missions and whatnot make them worthwile and fun and different enough from what you are doing in the main story that you don't feel like you are just repeating the same things.

By side missions I mean the missions to "tell the legend" of your weapons. The uruk missions do get repetitive but I still enjoyed the combat enough that I didn't mind doing them.

The "kill x number of uruks" missions get to be fairly easy once you get your bow to a point where you can recover your time slow bar (focus) and can combat drain for arrows. I spent most of those missions just going for the headshots, regenerating my focus and arrows, draining the nearest guy, and more headshots. So like I mentioned, the missions that don't just have you killing ♥♥♥♥ (like the ones that make you stealth kill enemies, or blow up enemies with a campfire while not being detected) are more fun.

It's a good, fun game if you like these types of things (think assassin creedy dealies) but a bit short if you only do the main story missions. So pad out your time with some of the side stuff.
