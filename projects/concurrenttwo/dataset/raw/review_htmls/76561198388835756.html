I loved this game! It's very calm, very relaxing, and very sweet. It's also very short, and I can see people complaining about the price per hour. In spoiler tags I'm going to talk about specifics that I enjoyed and things I would have liked. I will be mentioning the end of the game, so clicker beware.

I really enjoyed the story that unfolds as you move from home to home, and get a sense of who people are. Perhaps I simply missed the mechanic, but there were a few small items that I wished I could have clicked to figure out what they were (weird wooden thing wtf were you!). I felt personally attacked by the game when I started to pull out wrist braces, antacids, vitamins.

It ended a little too soon, and a little TOO sweetly, I think, which is not something I would typically say. I didn't want it to end in a devastating manner but in some ways having to later throw out the baby items because your teen's room is full and you don't have enough room for it all would be really impactful. There are more bittersweet moments that could have been had but weren't. I enjoyed how unique this was and it balanced perfectly on the edge of fun and tedium and kept enjoyable. Even when I was being silently lectured by red lines on where I put things, I didn't feel too upset (except for that damn wooden thing).

Overall I quite recommend this game, though for under 3 hours and an end that feels slightly flaccid, I struggle to agree that it's a $20 game. That said, I also think developers are probably woefully underpaid for the work they do, so I never know what to say about prices. Is it in line with others for the content-to-price? No. Is it really enjoyable, unique, and worth a buy? Yes.

Ahh, where to start? With myself, I suppose--I'm playing this game in the midst of the pandemic quarantine and what a perfect time for this game to come out. It's a relaxing, hopeful, but slightly melancholic game (I think I read that exact term in the marketing literature and they nailed it). This game is soothing to play and is exactly what I needed.

First off, the soundtrack is amazing. AMAZING. I always, always turn off game music. Don't need it, don't want it, get a headache. I kickstarted this game on a level without the OST, and I just went and bought the soundtrack today as well. It's stunning. Get the game if only to listen to the music. I also really enjoy the small sound effects within the game. This is like an ASMR snack. The art is gorgeous-I don't need to wax on about that because you can see for yourself on the store page just how fantastic the art is.

I will start with my favorite things: the game play is fast, and I never find myself waiting through loading screens, forced slow conversations, or cut scenes, which are the worst things in the world. Oh, and NO TUTORIAL. Hell, not having a tutorial gets five stars from me.

The other things I find fantastic about this game are things that some other players will not like. I'm not going to give major spoilers away, but stop reading if you don't want to know anything about game mechanics.

First off, the narrator is silent. In this game, you build conversations with a diverse, eccentric, sometimes enigmatic cast of characters, and you are the main character--but as the player you're just playing cards trying to develop some sort of conversation, you don't get to read the main characters responses. I find that absolutely stunning and refreshing---I'm developing the main character in my imagination. Her attitude, her responses, her attachment or distrust of each character, etc. But, some people will find the silent narrator frustrating.

This is an interactive visual novel, and people who come into this game looking for a card battler will leave disappointed. I do think the cards as a metaphor for conversation and connection with other people works, but it's a bit muffled, and I find myself more often than not forgetting that I'm building a conversation and I'm just trying to match symbols so I can get to the next snippet of conversation. I know it sounds tedious, and for some people it may be just that, but for me, uncovering each tidbit of conversation is a delight. The writing is good and several times I found myself quite taken by a turn of phrase--I didn't write it down but something like "the dogs tails whirring as fast as fans"--really fantastic imagery. I sometimes feel that there's a missing connection between the cards and the story of the game but, I can't verbalize what I'm looking for. There are special cards that include conversational tactics like preparation, or clarifications, but I don't know if they're actually having an effect on the story/responses.

Ahhh, and the piece that I'm not quite sure how to write about. In the game someone tells me this about my recently deceased mother: "Just because she had friends on the road doesn't mean you do. You don't inherit connections." I stopped short for a second when I got to this line because I realized this game isn't about a girl going on a road trip to fill her store; this is about people and the connections we make and dissolve during our lives. I mentioned in the beginning I'm playing this during the pandemic quarantine and this game hits all the feels. In this time of social distancing, here is a game about making connections to people, and the bittersweet realization that when you start to fit in better in some places, you start to lose pieces of the old you that fit in better somewhere else. The main character has a bit of a whoa moment when someone talks about her mother in a way that she's never heard her mother talked about before, and as the player we realize that this game is not just about people but also about our understanding of who someone is being challenged. This game is unintentionally timely and I cannot recommend it enough, for the people who are looking for that chill introspective moment in our current whirlwind climate. If you're expecting the next Slay the Spire or Undertale that's not what you'll find here, but for those who go in with an open mind I think you can find something especially poignant in all the things unsaid in the game.

I quite enjoyed this game, and if you're a fan of Amanita, then you'll like this little snack as well. Let's get the negatives out of the way right off--this game is playable in under an hour, and it's very simple.

For the things I enjoyed--the atmosphere (as I mentioned above, if you already know Amanita, then you know their "just this side of the circus" aesthetic and it works). The art is fantastic, the puzzles enjoyable, and the controls include a fast-forward so you never need to sit through something you've already seen. Fantastic. This game is also definitely replayable, as I finished and still hadn't unlocked a number of...events? secrets? Incidents, for lack of a better term. As you unlock little incidents due to the choices you make, you flip over cards. I had plenty of them still undiscovered when I finished the game. I don't know if you can do all the options in a single playthrough or whether it requires multiple. I am by no means a completionist so I'll probably never find out.

This game was so boring that I'm having a hard time formulating my review. Summary: This game is a complete and utter snoozefest. I requested a refund for my $2.50 sale price.

The gameplay is confusing at times (why are certain things greyed out? Who knows!), and the UI is poor in minor ways-- for example, some of the buttons that you click aren't animated, so you really have no idea when you've pressed them. It's not always clear where you're placing cards, or what options you have when selecting--another example is it shows you gold/silver/bronze cards and tells you to pick one, but then only gives you an option between gold and silver difficulty cards. It just doesn't make sense.

The multiplayer in this game is basically nonexistant too. You're in the game with the other person, but you don't interact with them..I'm still not sure if we were both playing together or competing against one another. I think it was a race to the end, but I'm honestly not sure. You're really just playing this board game on your own, and for me, it was just a series of bad fortune cards over, and over, and over, and over, and over, and over. We were so bored that at 1.4 hours in we agreed to just quit the game rather than suffer through any more. I don't recommend this to anyone. If you're really jonesing for a super long "multiplayer" board game, pick up Talisman.

This is your standard VN/dating sim game. Nothing earthshattering here--in fact, this may be one of the weaker ones I've played as you really don't get to make too many choices in the game. You don't even get to develop a personality for your character; Avery Grey is predetermined. The only thing you can change is your pronouns, and your look to a minor extent.

The story is quick. Like many romance games or novels it's TOO quick really; the plot advances so fast that there are gaping holes left when you've finished the story. But it's a cutesy story with an interesting twist (a twist that you may have gleaned just from reading the game blurbs or store page). It is absolutely nowhere near worth the $20 steam price however--I'd be furious to pay that kind of price for such amateur writing. If you're a completionist who wants to play every romance option in a VN, well, I can't give you any advice there as I can't imagine playing this a second time through. The pacing without options is too slow for me to even consider that. Expect an hour or two out of this game and call it good.

You may also want to stock up on coffee and jelly doughnuts before playing this game. Just trust me on that one.

Standard survival-management game. You have to balance health, hunger, depression, injury, exhaustion, and advance character stats, as is expected of the genre. Characters do have some back story and the game story itself is wacky. I'd insert a spoiler here about my opinion of the end but I'll be damned if I can get anywhere close to it--this game is not easy.

The overall tone of the game is a little on the desperate end, and the music is very somber. Characters are generally unpleasant, and why does every single map location have some sort of nasty RNG roll plus a little trick? This is a frustrating play for me--I enjoy it, but I don't feel like I get better each attempt. I'm not certain what I'm supposed to be learning.

This game is repetitive and difficult, and I do not recommend it at the $15 steam price, but I DO recommend it if you can snag it for a couple of bucks somewhere else. You can definitely waste a few hours playing; or if you're much better at the repetitive micro-management then I am, you may actually even beat the game.

Anyone familiar with This War of Mine or Dyscourse will find a similar package in different wrapping here--though this game has even fewer options/functions than the former two.
